AI-Driven Idea Generation

Part 2 of 5

Goal

Generate a diverse Idea Bank spanning MVP features, radical concepts, UX patterns, business-model tweaks, and distribution channels.

The Process

  1. Draft targeted prompts for different idea categories
  2. Run prompts and capture all outputs in your Idea Bank
  3. Iterate prompts to push into unexplored territories
  4. Highlight 3-4 most novel or surprising ideas

Prompt Library: Idea Generation

Use these prompts with NovaFit's problems, or adapt for your own context:

MVP Features: "Brainstorm 8 MVP features to solve [Problem 1: motivation drop-off]. Label each as either MVP or experiment."
Radical Concepts: "Suggest 5 radical product concepts that address [Problem 2: time constraints] in novel waysâ€”not just features, but reimagined approaches."
UX & Business Models: "Iterate: surface UX patterns, business-model tweaks, and channel experiments for [combined problems]. Think beyond the app itself."

Pro Tips

  • Be specific: Reference your actual problem statements
  • Request diversity: Explicitly ask for "novel," "unconventional," or "outside the box"
  • Iterate: If first output is too conservative, ask AI to "go further" or "challenge assumptions"

AI-Assisted Discovery Artifacts

Part 4 of 5

Goal

Produce one low-fidelity discovery artifact (prototype sketch, interview guide, survey, or journey map) to validate your top idea.

The Process

  1. Choose artifact type aligned to your hypothesis
  2. Draft with AI to create scaffold quickly
  3. Refine for context: Adjust tone, scope, specificity
  4. Peer feedback via "glow & grow" review
  5. Reflect on process: How did AI help? Where did you add value?

Artifact Scaffolding Prompts

Interview Guide

"Draft a 6-question interview guide to validate [idea]. Questions should probe behaviors, constraints, and success signals."

Survey

"Produce a low-fi survey with 6-8 items (mix multiple choice and open-ended) to validate [hypothesis]."

Journey Map

"Sketch a journey map for [persona] attempting [goal] in NovaFit. Include stages, tasks, emotions, breakpoints."

Prototype Sketch

"Describe a low-fi prototype for [feature]. Include key screens, user flows, and interactions to test."

AI Reflection & Ethical Guardrails

Part 5 of 5

Goal

Map the benefits vs. risks of AI across discovery phases, and co-create 2-3 actionable "if-then" guardrails for responsible use.

The Process

  1. Map value: Where did AI add speed, creativity, or scale?
  2. Map risks: Bias, blind spots, over-reliance, hallucinations
  3. Note impacts: What happens if AI assumptions are wrong?
  4. Draft guardrails: Concrete "if-then" rules with examples from your work

Benefits vs. Risks Across Discovery

âœ… Benefits

  • Speed: Hours â†’ minutes for pattern mining and ideation
  • Scale: Process 100s of inputs without human bottleneck
  • Creativity: Surface ideas outside your usual frame
  • Consistency: Apply same criteria across all ideas
  • Documentation: Auto-generate rationales and records

âš ï¸ Risks

  • Bias amplification: AI reflects patterns in training data
  • Blind spots: Missing cultural context or emerging signals
  • Over-reliance: Accepting AI output without critical review
  • Hallucinations: Plausible-sounding but false information
  • Homogenization: Converging on "safe" ideas that AI favors

Real-World Impact Example

Scenario: AI analysis of NovaFit feedback emphasizes "busy professionals" pain points because that segment is over-represented in your data.

Consequence: You build features that exclude stay-at-home parents, students, or retireesâ€”shrinking addressable market and missing diverse user needs.

Guardrail needed: Audit data sources for demographic balance before analysis.

Knowledge Check - Question 1 of 4

Ethical Guardrails

Which of the following is an effective "if-then" guardrail for AI-assisted discovery?

  • A) Always use AI for everything
  • B) Never trust AI outputs
  • C) If qualitative data lacks diversity, then oversample underrepresented groups before AI analysis
  • D) Only use AI for simple tasks

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Effective guardrails are specific, actionable, and address concrete risks. Option C provides a clear trigger condition and mitigation action.
